// 08 — FAQ

FREQUENTLY ASKED
QUESTIONS

Answers based on real EA logic and behavior, not generic marketing copy.

What exactly is a "run candle" and why does it matter?+
A run candle is a consecutive directional candle preceding a Boom or Crash spike. On Crash symbols, the price runs upward (green candles) before crashing. On Boom symbols, the price runs downward (red candles) before booming. VaultX counts these consecutive candles — the longer the run, the higher the probability of an imminent spike reversal. The entry threshold defines at what count the EA fires.
How does the layering system work exactly?+
After Layer 1 fires at your entry threshold (e.g. 9 candles), VaultX continues to monitor. Every N candles after that (the "Layer Step"), the EA opens another position — Layer 2, Layer 3, up to your Max Layers setting. Each layer enters at the current market price with its own SL, TP, breakeven, and trailing stop tracking. All layers are managed independently. When all layers close (via SL or TP), the EA resets and waits for the next spike setup.
What is the Fresh Run Guard and why is it important?+
The Fresh Run Guard prevents VaultX from entering partway through an already-in-progress candle run. Without it, the EA could enter at candle 6 of a run that started at candle 1 — missing the statistical edge entirely. VaultX waits for the run count to fall below the entry threshold, then re-arms. Every entry starts from a genuine fresh spike setup.
How does Compound Mode calculate the lot size?+
VaultX calculates: Risk Amount = Balance × Risk% ÷ 100. Then SL Cost Per Lot = (SL distance in price ÷ Tick Size) × Tick Value. Final Lot = Risk Amount ÷ SL Cost Per Lot. This is clamped between your Min Lot and Max Lot safety caps. The calculation uses live broker tick value data — so the dollar risk per trade stays accurate regardless of current price levels.
What does the trailing stop's "lock 70%" mean?+
VaultX tracks the highest profit percentage each layer has ever reached — called the "peak profit." The trailing SL is then set to Entry Price + (Peak Profit × Lock%). At 70% lock: if a trade peaks at +1.0%, the SL locks at +0.7% from entry. If the price reverses, the trade closes at +0.7% — locking in 70% of the peak gain. You keep most of the profit even if the spike reverses sharply.

Press "Apply Settings" and they take effect immediately — no need to detach, modify inputs, and re-attach the EA.

The Lifetime Access license includes full system access and updates. Each chart instance uses the same magic number by default — adjust MAGIC_NUMBER in inputs to differentiate instances if needed.

After every Strategy Tester run, VaultX writes one row to greedy_master_results.csv in your MT5 Common Files folder. The row includes: Symbol, Period, Entry Candles, SL%, TP%, Profit Factor, Drawdown %, Recovery Factor, Sharpe Ratio, Total Trades, Win Rate, Avg Win, Avg Loss, Net Profit, and Max Consecutive Losses. This plugs directly into the Google Sheets optimization tracker.
